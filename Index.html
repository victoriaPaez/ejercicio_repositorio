MOTHERBOARD:

The motherboard is an electronic circuit that allows the physical connection of all the components of the computer it has software called the BIOS, allowing the data communication, control, monitoring, administration or power management electrical, timing, and computer synchronism.

The motherboard has the necessary connectors for the processor, RAM, ports and video cards or the network card, USB port, parallel port, firewire port and serial port, Network port, and PS /2 type.

MOUSE:

The mouse is a peripheral that allows the interaction of the computer with the user, by a mechanism electronics that detects movement in the axis (x and z) known as the lateral translation (from right to left), is visualized on the computer screen in the form of a pointer, arrow or cursor.

The mouse has two buttons:

The left one is for opening, dragging, selecting, and executing functions.

The right allows you to access additional functions of the commands. Most mice have a central wheel known as a scroll. With it, you can scroll through documents from top to bottom and vice versa

KEYBOARD:

The keyboard is a peripheral that allows data to be entered a computer, are classified into six sections:

Function: located at the on the top keyboard represented from the key F1 to F12 doing a specific function to access shortcuts in programs.
Control: access commands or perform certain actions, the most used on computers with Windows operating system are Ctrl, Alt, Esc, and the windows icon, and with macOS X operating system the most used is Command.
Alphanumeric: are the keys represented for letters, numbers, symbols, and punctuation marks
Numeric: used to enter numerical data, they are grouped in the same way as they appear in calculators.
Specials and displacement: they are used to move through documents, web pages, and edit some text such as Delete, Start, End, Page Up, Page Down, ImpPt, and the arrow keys.

GRAPHICS CARD:

The graphics card is a peripheral device, is integrated into the motherboard of the computer, and is dedicated to the processing of binary data (one and zero) related to video and images, transforming them into visual information, there are two types of graphics cards.


The integrated ones that are coupled to the processor have fairly low power, needing to take part in the system's RAM for its operation. The dedicated provide a level of performance far superior to integrated graphics, they have their own graphics memory, video outputs, and cooling system, and are connected to the motherboard through the PCI-Express port.

SCREEN:

The Screen is a device electronic that it shows the information in the form of image and text, which is generated by the video card, the function of the monitor is to show the images or text through its basic unit called a pixel, the more pixels in a screen, its quality will be higher because it will have higher resolution.


There are several types of monitors. Some are large, such as a box with cathode-ray tube technology, which allows images to be viewed using a cathode ray beam, and others some are quite thin that use a liquid crystal display, a light-emitting diode, or plasma.

QUALITY:

To achieve software quality, concordance with explicitly established functional and performance requirements must be achieved, with explicitly documented development standards and with the implicit characteristics expected of all software, defined as:

Software requirements are the foundations from which quality is measured. The lack of concordance with the requirements is a lack of quality. The specified standards define a set of development criteria that guides how software engineering is applied; If certain criteria are not followed, there will almost always be a lack of quality.


There is a set of implicit requirements (the desire for good maintenance). If the software conforms to your requirements but fails to meet the implicit requirements of the software you are left in between.

QUALITY STANDARDS:

A quality standard is a reference product that facilitates the task of setting minimum conditions so that the aspects and characteristics of a product, in this case a multimedia editorial product, efficiently meet the needs of its users. The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) defines these standards as documents established by council and approved by a recognized institution that provides, for common and repeated use, rules, guidelines and characteristics for activities or their results, aimed at achieving the optimum degree definition in a given context.


In addition, the quality standard provides information on the modifications to be made to the product to improve the process or service offered to the end user.

DATA SYNCHRONIZATION

Data synchronization is a requirement in information systems, is the process of alignment between the data from various sources, the coordination of processes that are executed simultaneously, to obtain a correct order of execution and avoid system errors, keeping them In the same version, changes made to any file will be reflected in all other devices can be computers, telephones, and other computing devices.


This action allows obtaining accurate and reliable data from all systems, be they operational or transactional. Thus, each organization will be able to create its logic for the synchronization of its data in real-time, if required, obtaining greater precision, consistency of information, and a substantial improvement in performance.
